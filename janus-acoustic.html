Acoustic Physics

Snell's Law & Refraction.

Traditional radio waves (RF) attenuate and die almost immediately when striking seawater. To communicate with subsurface Remotely Operated Vehicles (ROVs) or deep-sea sensors, OMEGA must abandon the electromagnetic spectrum and utilize mechanical energy: acoustic waves.

However, marine acoustics face severe physical physics challenges. As the water's temperature, salinity, and depth (pressure) change, the physical speed of sound shifts. According to Snell's Law of Refraction, sound waves do not travel in straight lines underwater; they aggressively bend toward regions of slower sound speed.

Relevance: This refraction creates massive acoustic "shadow zones" where communication is physically impossible, even if the ROV is only a few hundred meters away. OMEGA's acoustic processing algorithms account for these extreme environmental variables when predicting mesh connectivity.

NATO Protocols

The JANUS Bridge.

To combat the severe multipath fading and echo interference caused by sound bouncing wildly off the ocean floor and the surface waves, OMEGA implements the NATO-standard JANUS acoustic protocol (STANAG 4748).

How it works: The gateway translates standardized JSON mesh commands into Frequency-Shift Keyed (FSK) acoustic waveforms. It treats a massive hardware hydrophone exactly like it treats a standard terrestrial radio interface.

By using the JANUS specification, OMEGA nodes can inherently interoperate with allied naval vessels, autonomous gliders, and university acoustic modems right out of the box, breaking proprietary vendor lock-in in the underwater communications space.

Acoustic Subsea Frames

JANUS Initialization.

Because underwater acoustic physics are incredibly hostile, bandwidth is severely limited—often maxing out at a mere 80 bits per second (bps). Therefore, OMEGA cannot stream complex JSON over a hydrophone.

How it works: The janus-announce.json structure represents the simulated NATO-standard JANUS initialization frame. The payload explicitly defines the center_frequency_hz and the rx_level_db. The Gateway uses this payload to align the hydrophone's physical oscillator. Once aligned, the actual data transferred via the acoustic bridge is strictly limited to heavily compressed initialization vectors and critical emergency stop commands, preserving the fragile acoustic channel.
