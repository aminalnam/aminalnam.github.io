How OMEGA Works.

A learning path to understand the data lifecycle from physical sensor to digital map.

The Learning Path.

1. Data Acquisition

Understand how Sonar pings, GPS coordinates, and IMU pitch/roll data are synchronized on the edge device.

2. Quality Control

Learn how OMEGA flags anomalies, filtering out bad GPS fixes and acoustic multi-path errors.

3. Interpolation (IDW)

Understand how Inverse Distance Weighting fills the gaps between physical survey lines to create a continuous surface.

4. Geospatial Export

See how the backend compiles the grid into standard KML/KMZ and GeoTIFF formats for GIS software.

Glossaries.

Quality Flags

OMEGA follows standard oceanographic flagging: 1 (Pass), 2 (Not Evaluated), 3 (Suspect), 4 (Fail), 9 (Missing Data).

Sensor Glossary

HDOP: Horizontal Dilution of Precision (GPS accuracy).
IMU: Inertial Measurement Unit.
Pitch/Roll: The tilt of the vessel, which changes the angle of the sonar beam.
IDW: Inverse Distance Weighting, a spatial interpolation math.
