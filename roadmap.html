Data Science & Cartography

Hexbinned Aggregation (H3 Grid).

As the OMEGA network scales globally—incorporating thousands of autonomous nodes, millions of historical pings, and massive public NDBC datasets—rendering individual geospatial points causes severe UI map clutter and crashes the browser's DOM renderer.

The planned roadmap includes migrating the entire spatial database to Uber's H3 Hexagonal Hierarchical Spatial Index. Instead of plotting individual coordinate nodes, the map will plot discrete, hierarchical hexagonal geographic buckets. These hexagons will be dynamically color-coded by the statistical median or standard deviation of the millions of nodes contained within them.

Relevance: This allows an operator to zoom out to view the entire Pacific Ocean as a massive, low-resolution hex-grid heatmap, and seamlessly zoom in to a single coral reef to view individual node resolutions, without ever overwhelming the WebGL engine.

Edge AI

Machine Learning Inferencing (TinyML).

Currently, edge nodes rely entirely on the shore Gateway to process complex data and detect anomalies. Future fleet iterations will push Machine Learning Inferencing (TinyML) directly onto the ESP32 and ESP32-S3 edge microcontrollers.

How it works: By training a highly compressed TensorFlow Lite model on thousands of hours of baseline vibration profiles and hydrophone acoustic data, the edge node will be able to detect complex mechanical anomalies locally. For example, if biofouling occludes the salinity probe, the vibration frequency shifts. The TinyML model detects this shift instantly and transmits a high-priority 50-byte alarm over LoRa, triggering a deep-sleep cycle to preserve the remaining battery, without ever needing to transmit the massive raw vibration matrix to the cloud.
