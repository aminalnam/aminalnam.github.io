Embedded C++

T-Beam Supreme Architecture.

Located in firmware/tbeam_supreme_dual_sensor/, the C++ codebase is highly optimized for the ESP32-S3 microcontroller. The firmware aggressively manages deep-sleep power states, shutting down the GNSS (GPS) module and the Semtech SX1262 LoRa radio between transmission windows.

Core

ESP32-S3

Dual-core XTensa LX7 @ 240MHz. Handles CBOR compression and mesh logic.

RF Transceiver

Semtech SX1262

LoRa CSS modulation. Capable of +22dBm output for over-the-horizon links.

Location

u-blox MAX-M10S

Ultra-low-power GNSS. Acquires hot-fixes in <2 seconds before sleeping.

Thermodynamics: A remote sensor floating in the Pacific cannot plug into a wall outlet. The ESP32-S3 draws roughly 100mA while active, draining a standard 18650 lithium battery in two days. By forcing the hardware into the ULP (Ultra Low Power) sleep state, the draw drops to 15µA.

Active Cooling

Thermal Management & TX-Burst Fan Control.

For extreme-range shore nodes utilizing the T-Beam 1W or custom High Power Amplifiers (HPA) pushing +30 dBm, passive cooling is insufficient. Sustained transmissions at 1 Watt of RF power will rapidly thermally throttle or permanently damage the silicon.

OMEGA firmware implements dynamic TX-Burst Fan Control. Utilizing maximum GPIO drive strength, the ESP32-S3 physically spins up an external cooling fan seconds before a scheduled transmission burst, and spins it down immediately after the LoRa packet clears the antenna. This active thermal management guarantees silicon safety during full-power mesh operation while strictly preserving the power budget during deep sleep.

Robotic Integration

Mapping ROV Interoperability.

The firmware/mapping_rov_mega/ and mapping_rov_esp32/ profiles bridge the gap between surface telemetry and deep-water Remotely Operated Vehicles. The C++ code parses high-frequency sonar ping data via serial from the ROV's internal control unit and dynamically maps it to the standardized SenML/CBOR envelope.

This approach allows an underwater ROV to stream structural maps and internal battery status directly to the shore gateway via a surface tethered relay.

firmware/mapping_rov_esp32/src/sonar.cpp 
void process_sonar_ping(PingData* ping) {
    // 1. Read raw acoustic return from UART
    float depth = calculate_tof_depth(ping->time_of_flight);
    
    // 2. Package into SenML Observation
    Observation obs = {
        .metric_id = METRIC_SONAR_DEPTH,
        .value = depth,
        .timestamp = current_rtc_time()
    };
    
    // 3. Compress to CBOR & enqueue for RF
    enqueue_cbor_payload(&obs);
}
Delay Tolerant Networking

LoRa Relay Nodes.

The firmware/lora_relay_node/ profile acts purely as a transparent, "dumb" repeater in the Delay Tolerant Network. By stripping away all sensor and GNSS logic, the firmware focuses 100% of CPU time on listening for faint LoRa chirps.

These nodes execute the omega_lora_protocol.h collision-avoidance (CSMA-CA) algorithms to catch weak packets bouncing over the waves, buffer them in RAM, and cleanly blast them out at +22dBm, effectively extending the mesh network's range over the horizon.

Energy Physics

Solar Budgets & Insolation.

A remote node floating in the North Atlantic during winter might only receive 2 hours of direct solar insolation per day. The node must mathematically budget its stored energy (joules) to survive a 14-hour night.

Dark Current

The Silent Killer

Even when not actively transmitting, a spinning ESP32 processor draws a "dark current" of 100mA. Overnight, this will completely drain the lithium cell, causing the physical hardware to freeze.

Deep Sleep

15µA Survival

The OMEGA firmware physically cuts the MOSFET power rail to the GPS and LoRa chips, dropping the entire board into a 15 micro-amp deep sleep state between transmissions.

Dynamic Polling

Battery-Aware Rates

If the battery voltage drops below 3.6V, the firmware automatically downscales its sample rate from every 10 minutes to every 60 minutes, mathematically guaranteeing survival until sunrise.
