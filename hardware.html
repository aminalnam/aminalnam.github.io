PCB Design

RF Trace Impedance

When designing the Printed Circuit Board (PCB) for an OMEGA node, routing the signal from the Semtech SX1262 pin to the SMA antenna connector is not as simple as drawing a copper line. At 915 MHz, electricity behaves as a wave, not a direct current.

50-Ohm Matching

Transmission Lines

To prevent the RF wave from bouncing off the antenna and reflecting back into the chip (VSWR), the copper trace must have exactly a 50-ohm characteristic impedance. We calculate the trace width (e.g., 0.3mm) and the dielectric spacing to the ground plane beneath it using coplanar waveguide equations.

Pi-Matching Network

Inductors & Capacitors

Even with perfect trace width, parasitic capacitance exists. We place a Pi-Network (two capacitors and one inductor) immediately before the SMA connector. Using a Vector Network Analyzer (VNA), we physically tune these components to ensure maximum power transfer to the antenna.

Sensor Wiring

I2C Bus Capacitance

The I2C protocol was designed for communication between chips on the same circuit board (Inter-Integrated Circuit). OMEGA physically abuses this protocol by running I2C cables 5 meters down into the ocean to reach benthic sensors.

The Mathematics of Pull-Up Resistors 
As the sensor cable gets longer, the physical copper wire acts as a capacitor. 
When the ESP32 tries to send a digital HIGH (3.3V) or LOW (0V) square wave, the 
capacitance smooths the wave into a slow curve. If the curve is too slow, the 
sensor fails to read the bit.

The Solution: Stronger Pull-Up Resistors.
Standard short-wire I2C uses 10kΩ resistors. For a 5-meter ocean drop, the 
capacitance approaches 400pF (the absolute limit of the I2C spec). 
OMEGA mathematically lowers the pull-up resistance to exactly 2.2kΩ. 
This allows more current to rapidly charge the parasitic cable capacitance, 
snapping the square waves back into sharp, readable logic levels.
Embedded C++

T-Beam Supreme Architecture.

Located in firmware/tbeam_supreme_dual_sensor/, the C++ codebase is highly optimized for the ESP32-S3 microcontroller. The firmware aggressively manages deep-sleep power states, shutting down the GNSS (GPS) module and the Semtech SX1262 LoRa radio between transmission windows.

Core

ESP32-S3

Dual-core XTensa LX7 @ 240MHz. Handles TLV compression and mesh logic.

RF Transceiver

Semtech SX1262

LoRa CSS modulation. Capable of +22dBm output for over-the-horizon links.

Location

u-blox MAX-M10S

Ultra-low-power GNSS. Acquires hot-fixes in <2 seconds before sleeping.

Thermodynamics: A remote sensor floating in the Pacific cannot plug into a wall outlet. The ESP32-S3 draws roughly 100mA while active, draining a standard 18650 lithium battery in two days. By forcing the hardware into the ULP (Ultra Low Power) sleep state, the draw drops to 15µA.
