Radio Physics & Information Theory

The Shannon-Hartley Limit.

Marine environments are notoriously hostile to radio frequency (RF) propagation. The ocean acts as a dynamic ground plane, and atmospheric salt creates significant signal attenuation. According to the Shannon-Hartley theorem, the maximum data rate of a communication channel is strictly bound by its bandwidth and the signal-to-noise ratio (SNR).

To overcome this, OMEGA-wave utilizes LoRa Chirp Spread Spectrum (CSS) modulation. By spreading the signal across a wider bandwidth, the system can decode packets below the noise floor.

Graph Theory & Delay Tolerant Networks (DTN)

Traditional IP networks assume a connected path. OMEGA-wave uses a store-and-forward DTN overlay. If an ASV cannot reach the shore, it holds the packet in memory until it physically drifts within range of a relay buoy.

Acoustic Physics

The JANUS Protocol.

Radio waves attenuate almost immediately in seawater. To communicate with subsurface ROVs, OMEGA-wave utilizes mechanical energy: acoustic waves.

However, acoustics face severe physical challenges. As temperature and salinity change with depth, the speed of sound shifts. According to Snell's Law of Refraction, sound waves bend toward regions of slower sound speed, creating "shadow zones" and multipath fading.

OMEGA-wave implements the NATO-standard JANUS acoustic protocol bridge to survive multipath fading.

Acoustic Bridge Architecture

The gateway translates JSON mesh commands into standard acoustic waveforms, treating a hydrophone exactly like a terrestrial radio.
