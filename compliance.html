The Legal Framework

Legal & Compliance.

Navigating international maritime law, RF regulations, and the constraints of operating autonomous hardware in the ocean.

Radio Frequency Law

FCC vs ITU Regulations

You cannot simply blast a 1-Watt radio signal over the ocean without consequences. OMEGA operates in the license-free Industrial, Scientific, and Medical (ISM) radio bands, but these bands are heavily regulated by regional governments.

United States (FCC Part 15)

915 MHz

In the US, the FCC allows transmission in the 902-928 MHz band up to 1 Watt (30 dBm) provided the system utilizes Frequency Hopping Spread Spectrum (FHSS) or Digital Modulation. OMEGA's LoRa Chirp Spread Spectrum satisfies this, allowing for aggressive, high-power long-distance transmissions without strict time-on-air limits.

Europe (ETSI / ITU)

868 MHz & 1% Duty Cycle

In Europe, the ETSI regulations are brutally strict. You are limited to 25 mW (14 dBm) and a 1% Duty Cycle. This means a buoy can only transmit for 36 seconds per hour. The OMEGA firmware dynamically detects its GPS region and mathematically throttles the CSMA-CA queue to ensure it never violates the legal duty cycle limit.

Maritime Law

UNCLOS & Drifting Sensors

The United Nations Convention on the Law of the Sea (UNCLOS) governs all activities in the ocean. Operating unmanned autonomous hardware introduces significant legal gray areas.

Data Ownership: Under UNCLOS, marine scientific research conducted within a nation's Exclusive Economic Zone (EEZ) requires the consent of the coastal state. Because OMEGA nodes are free-drifting, ocean currents routinely push them across international borders. The platform automatically tags all telemetry with high-precision GNSS coordinates, allowing researchers to mathematically filter out data collected in restricted geopolitical zones to remain compliant.

Hazards to Navigation: A major legal liability for autonomous buoys is ship strikes. A fiberglass yacht striking a massive steel buoy can cause catastrophic damage and loss of life. OMEGA nodes mitigate this legal liability entirely through their physical design: by utilizing lightweight PVC, FDM plastics, and a breakaway elastic mooring, the buoy will simply shatter harmlessly if struck by a vessel, protecting the mariners at the expense of the $200 hardware.

Data Privacy

GDPR & PII Anonymization

When deploying sensors near populated coastlines, edge nodes inevitably sweep up incidental Personally Identifiable Information (PII), such as MAC addresses from passing smartphones or AIS transponders from private yachts.

gateway/security/anonymization.py 
import hashlib

def anonymize_mac_address(mac_address: str, daily_salt: str) -> str:
    """
    To comply with GDPR, raw MAC addresses sniffed by the edge node 
    are immediately one-way hashed before ever hitting the cloud database.
    A rotating daily salt ensures that long-term tracking of individuals 
    is cryptographically impossible.
    """
    payload = f"{mac_address}:{daily_salt}".encode('utf-8')
    # Generate a SHA-256 hash and truncate to save database space
    return hashlib.sha256(payload).hexdigest()[:16]

# Example: Sniffed MAC address "00:1B:44:11:3A:B7"
anonymized_id = anonymize_mac_address("00:1B:44:11:3A:B7", "SALT_2026-06-01")
print(f"Stored ID: {anonymized_id}") # Stored ID: c6b4a3a...
