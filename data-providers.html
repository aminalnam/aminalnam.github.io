Oceanographic Formats

ERDDAP & NetCDF Structures.

Global oceanographic datasets (Sea Surface Temperature, Salinity) are distributed in NetCDF (Network Common Data Form) files. These are massive multidimensional arrays plotting latitude, longitude, depth, and time.

The OMEGA `erddap.py` adapter queries remote ERDDAP federations, parses tabular payloads, and automatically projects them into standardized GeoJSON so the UI can instantly render massive external deployments alongside native OMEGA mesh data.

Satellite Physics

Global Map Fusion.

To measure Sea Surface Temperature (SST), satellites use radiometers to detect thermal infrared radiation. To model ocean currents, they use radar altimetry—bouncing microwave pulses off the ocean surface to measure minute differences in sea surface height.

The Gateway acts as a caching proxy for these external APIs, normalizing the outputs to prevent exhausting rate limits.

Fisheries & Security

Global Fishing Watch (GFW).

OMEGA integrates directly with the Global Fishing Watch 4Wings API. It pulls real-time heatmap MVT overlays for apparent fishing effort, AIS vessel presence, and Synthetic Aperture Radar (SAR) detections of "dark targets" (vessels not transmitting AIS).

Furthermore, the gateway pulls vessel risk and IUU (Illegal, Unreported, and Unregulated) insights, matching detected AIS tracks against global registries to flag vessels fishing within no-take Marine Protected Areas (MPAs).

Maritime Traffic

Live Commercial AIS & MarineCadastre.

For live tactical awareness, the gateway ingests commercial AIS streams (via AISStream), tracking thousands of live vessels and superimposing them onto the OMEGA Map Portal in real-time.

For historical context, the portal registers U.S. MarineCadastre transit-count overlays. These are cached ArcGIS tiles displaying annual vessel-density heatmaps (2019-2025), allowing operators to plan autonomous survey routes that actively avoid historical high-traffic shipping lanes.

Climate Models

NASA EarthData & Copernicus.

The gateway integrates NASA EarthData CMR-STAC endpoints and the Copernicus Marine catalog. This allows OMEGA to query multi-petabyte federated cloud datasets, pulling specific climate variables (like Chlorophyll-a concentrations from Sentinel-3) to compare against real-time, local water-quality buoy data.

Public Ingestion

NOAA NDBC & CO-OPS Public Loader.

Rather than relying strictly on proprietary hardware, OMEGA utilizes a Public Loader daemon. It actively scrapes NOAA National Data Buoy Center (NDBC) buoys, CO-OPS tide/met products, and Open-Meteo marine models.

Crucially, it normalizes these diverse public sources into the exact same Observation Envelope schema used by OMEGA hardware, allowing the frontend to render public buoys interchangeably with private fleet assets.

Maritime Safety

AISHub & MarineTraffic Fallback.

If the local VHF AIS receiver fails, the gateway features built-in fallback integrations for aishub.py, marinetraffic.py, and spire_maritime.py. This triple-redundant architecture ensures the autonomous fleet always has line-of-sight to commercial supertankers traversing the survey polygon, executing emergency collision-avoidance maneuvers via the active_guard.py daemon.

Subsurface Profiles

Argo Float Calibration.

Surface sensors only tell half the story. The argo_calibration.py and argovis.py modules query the global Argo network (a fleet of 4,000 autonomous profiling floats). OMEGA cross-references the CTD (Conductivity, Temperature, Depth) casts from nearby Argo floats to automatically calibrate the on-board thermistors of the ASV as it transits across the thermocline.

Ecological Monitoring

NOAA Coral Reef Watch.

By integrating coral_reef_watch.py, OMEGA ingests 5km satellite virtual stations to monitor degree heating weeks (DHW). When the gateway detects a marine heatwave stress alert, it automatically redirects the ASV fleet to the affected reef coordinates to capture high-resolution acoustic bathymetry and dissolved oxygen samples before a mass bleaching event occurs.

Hydrodynamics

HFRNet Surface Currents.

Navigating an ASV against a 4-knot current drains the battery instantly. The hfrnet.py module ingests the High-Frequency Radar Network data, providing real-time vector fields of ocean surface currents. The wayfinding.py module consumes this vector matrix, executing A* pathfinding that "surfs" the currents, drastically extending operational range.

Biodiversity

OBIS Marine Species Tracking.

The Ocean Biodiversity Information System (obis.py) integration allows the gateway to pull historical acoustic tag detections. When the fleet operates in known migration corridors (e.g., Great White Shark breeding grounds), the gateway lowers the propulsion RPM to reduce acoustic signatures and minimize ecological disturbance.

Emergency Egress

National Hurricane Center Tracking.

The nhc.py daemon constantly polls the National Hurricane Center for active cyclone cones. If a deployed gateway or floating LoRa relay intersects with the 48-hour probability cone of a Category 3+ storm, the Mission Portal throws a catastrophic weather alarm and automatically commands all ASVs to return-to-base or dive to a safe depth.
