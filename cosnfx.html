The engineering of a flawless hero prop. A Wear OS IMU-driven kinetic synthesizer with 1.4ms audio latency.

At A Glance

Distributed Master/Drone Architecture

In professional stage magic, a sound effect that fires 50ms late ruins the immersion. CosNFX is an Android Wear OS application that transforms a smartwatch into a flawless sensory instrument, delivering instant acoustic and visual feedback with the same reliability as a physical drum.

Audio Engineering

The Latency Wall.

Standard Android audio routing has a round-trip latency of 40ms to 100ms. For a kinetic prop triggered by a physical wrist snap, this delay is completely unacceptable.

To break the latency wall, the system completely bypasses the standard high-level media players. The audio engine is written to stream raw mono PCM data directly into the hardware's AudioTrack. By aggressively optimizing the internal buffer down to just 64 samples, the system achieves a hardware response time of ~1.4ms. This guarantees that the sound of a "Laser Blast" erupts from the speaker at the precise micro-moment the user's wrist hits its maximum velocity peak.

Sensor Fusion

Solving the Ghost Trigger

Early prototypes relied solely on arm orientation, which resulted in "ghost triggers" every time the user gestured naturally while talking. The "Ironclad" Kinetic Engine was built to solve this via math.

Kinematics

Angular Jerk

Instead of looking at gravity, the engine fuses the Gyroscope to detect Angular Velocity (the "snap") and Linear Accelerometers to detect G-force Jerk (the physical "impact" at the end of a punch).

State Machines

3-Tier Posture Gate

While the arm is resting (RELAXED), the sensors are electronically muted to save battery. Only when the arm is raised into a combat posture (READY) does the high-frequency polling loop prime the system for a STRIKE.

Orchestration

The Surround Synthesis Paradox.

Wireless networks introduce unpredictable jitter. When attempting to trigger sounds simultaneously on both a phone and a watch, the phone will always react faster. Rather than fighting this fundamental law of physics, the system engineers it into a feature.

Surround Synthesis: The phone instantly triggers a high-frequency "Ignition" transient sound. A split-second later, the watch delivers the deep "Core Impact" audio directly from the hand. The result is a cinematic 3D presence where power audibly sounds like it is "charging up" in the user's chest and "erupting" physically from their hand.
