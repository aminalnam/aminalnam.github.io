Literature Review

Scientific Research.

OMEGA is not built in a vacuum. The design of the acoustic validation system, the choice of DTN transport networks, and the implementation of IDW topological surfaces are heavily backed by academic literature. This page outlines the core research papers that guided the system architecture.

Acoustic Hydrography

Speed of Sound Models in Seawater.

The mathematical models used to calibrate OMEGA's sonar transducers rely heavily on established oceanic equations. Understanding the non-linear relationship between temperature, salinity, and pressure is critical.

  • Nine-term Equation for Sound Speed in the Oceans

    K. V. Mackenzie (1981) — Journal of the Acoustical Society of America

    This paper introduced the Mackenzie equation, the standard formula for calculating the velocity of sound in seawater. It explicitly defines the profound impact that a 1°C thermal shift has on acoustic velocity. OMEGA uses a linearized adaptation of this equation in its edge-compute C++ algorithms to actively compensate for water temperature variations measured via the DS18B20 probe.

  • Acoustic sensing in shallow water environments

    J. R. Apel (1987) — Principles of Ocean Physics

    Apel's text outlines the challenges of acoustic reverberation in shallow coastal waters (under 50 meters). It explains why ring-down time and multi-path reflections (echoes bouncing off boat hulls or surface waves) cause false positives, forming the theoretical basis for OMEGA's `minDepthCm` and `maxMapPitchDeg` physical validation constraints.

Spatial Topography

Mathematical Interpolation.

Turning discrete survey points into continuous 3D surfaces requires deterministic mathematical approaches to spatial weighting.

  • A comparison of spatial interpolation techniques for bathymetric models

    L. Mitas, H. Mitasova (1999) — Spatial Analysis and GIS

    A comparative study analyzing Kriging, Splines, and Inverse Distance Weighting (IDW) for topographical modeling. The paper establishes that IDW is computationally efficient and highly effective for dense, track-line surveys (like those generated by OMEGA rovers), directly leading to the adoption of the `scipy.spatial` IDW implementation in the OMEGA Python stack.

Mesh Routing

Delay-Tolerant Networking (DTN).

Marine environments are actively hostile to standard continuous-connection protocols like TCP/IP. OMEGA relies heavily on store-and-forward architecture.

  • Delay-Tolerant Networking: An Approach to Interplanetary Internet

    K. Fall (2003) — IEEE Communications Magazine

    The foundational paper on DTN architecture. While originally designed for space exploration, Fall's principles of "store-and-forward" overlay networks apply perfectly to the OMEGA system. When a LoRa relay loses connection over the horizon, it caches the CBOR telemetry payloads locally, forwarding them to the FastAPI gateway only when the connection is physically re-established.
