RF Physics

LoRa Propagation Over Water

LoRa in Marine Environments: A Measurement Campaign

Petajajarvi, J., et al. (2017). IEEE International Symposium on Medical Information and Communication Technology.

Application to OMEGA: This paper proves that LoRa CSS modulation is exceptionally resilient to multipath fading caused by wave reflections over saltwater. OMEGA utilizes their findings to justify the use of 915MHz bands for over-the-horizon buoy-to-shore links, rather than relying on expensive satellite telemetry.

Fresnel Zone Clearance in Low Power Wide Area Networks

Augustin, A., et al. (2016). Sensors Journal.

Application to OMEGA: The OMEGA Gateway tower placement strategy relies on this paper's formulas for calculating the 60% clearance of the first Fresnel zone. Because saltwater is a near-perfect RF reflector, maintaining height above the ocean surface is critical to prevent destructive interference.

Marine Biology

Biofouling & Benthic Science

NOAA QARTOD Manual for Real-Time Quality Control

U.S. Integrated Ocean Observing System (IOOS). (2020).

Application to OMEGA: The entire backend Analytics Pipeline and Quality Control flag system (Pass, Suspect, Fail, Missing) is directly modeled on the NOAA QARTOD standards for In-Situ Temperature and Salinity data. This ensures OMEGA datasets are statistically valid for academic marine science.

Marine Biofouling: A Global Review

Fitridge, I., et al. (2012). Biofouling Journal.

Application to OMEGA: OMEGA's mechanical engineering decisions—specifically the use of copper-infused antifouling paints and mechanical wipers on the optical sensors—are derived from this review of biofilm progression (from diatoms to barnacles) in photic ocean zones.

Edge AI & Telemetry

Machine Learning & Compression

TensorFlow Lite Micro: Embedded Machine Learning

David, R., et al. (2021). Proceedings of Machine Learning Research.

Application to OMEGA: This foundational Google paper outlines the architecture of running quantized neural networks on resource-constrained microcontrollers. OMEGA utilizes TFLite Micro on the ESP32-S3 for "Phase 4" Over-The-Air (OTA) Machine Learning model execution.

Data Compression for IoT: A First-Order Predictive Approach

Srisooksai, T., et al. (2012). IEEE Communications Surveys & Tutorials.

Application to OMEGA: Because LoRa payloads are strictly limited to 250 bytes, OMEGA uses the mathematical concepts in this paper to implement its Lossless Predictive Coding algorithm. By transmitting only residual deltas, the network increases sensor yield by 10x per packet.
