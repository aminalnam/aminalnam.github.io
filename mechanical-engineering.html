Buoyancy Physics

Archimedes' Principle

A floating sensor node is a delicate balance of mass and volume. If the hull is too small, the weight of the lithium batteries will sink it. If the hull is too large, the wind drag will snap the anchor chain.

The Math

Displacement Volume

According to Archimedes, the upward buoyant force exerted on a body is equal to the weight of the fluid it displaces. Because saltwater is denser than freshwater (1,025 kg/m³), an OMEGA hull technically floats higher in the ocean than in a test tank. We mathematically model the exact cubic centimeter volume of the 3D-printed hull against the 250-gram payload of the battery and logic board to ensure the solar panel sits exactly 3 inches above the waterline.

Buoyancy Force Equation 
Fb = ρ × V × g

Where:
  Fb = Buoyant Force (Newtons)
  ρ = Density of Saltwater (1,025 kg/m³)
  V = Displaced Volume of the Hull (m³)
  g = Gravity (9.81 m/s²)

To float, Fb must be strictly greater than the total mass of the 
buoy multiplied by gravity. For OMEGA:
Mass = 0.25 kg
Target Displaced Volume = 0.0003 m³ (300 cm³)
Fb = 1025 * 0.0003 * 9.81 = 3.01 Newtons of upward force.
Gravity Force (Weight) = 0.25 * 9.81 = 2.45 Newtons.
Result: 3.01N > 2.45N (Positive Buoyancy)
The Center of Gravity

Ballast Design

A buoy that flips upside down in a wave will instantly lose GPS lock, LoRa telemetry, and solar charging. To prevent this, the heavy 18650 lithium cells are physically mounted at the absolute bottom of the PVC hull, acting as a pendulum ballast to maintain a low Center of Gravity (CG) and force the buoy to self-right within seconds.

Manufacturing

Marine Sealing (IP67)

Standard 3D-printed plastics are microscopically porous. If you submerge a raw 3D-printed hull, capillary action will draw saltwater straight through the plastic walls and instantly destroy the electronics.

The Solution: OMEGA hulls are printed using Fused Deposition Modeling (FDM) with PETG filament (which resists UV degradation better than PLA). Once printed, the hulls are aggressively sanded and painted with a Two-Part Marine Epoxy Resin (e.g., West System). This epoxy chemically bonds to the plastic, filling all microscopic layer lines and creating a completely hermetic, IP67-rated monolithic glass shell. Cable penetrations for the thermistor and hydrophone are sealed using marine-grade polyurethane sealants (like 3M 5200) that remain flexible under extreme pressure.

Kinetic Energy

Mooring Dynamics

When a 3-meter ocean swell hits a buoy, it transfers a massive amount of kinetic energy. If the buoy is anchored to the seafloor with a rigid rope, that kinetic energy has nowhere to go.

Elastic Rodes: To survive high sea states, OMEGA nodes utilize an elastic mooring strategy. The upper 10 meters of the anchor line consists of heavy-duty marine bungee cord. When the wave crests, the bungee stretches, absorbing the shock load and allowing the buoy to effortlessly ride *over* the wave, rather than being ripped violently under the surface.
